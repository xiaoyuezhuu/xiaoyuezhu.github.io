About

I am recent neuroscience PhD from New York University. The title of my thesis is "Analyses of decision under risk in rats". Originally from China, I majored in neuroscience in University of St. Andrews in Scotland, and published a paper on the motorneurons of tadpoles with Dr. Wenchang Li. I also exchanged one year abroad in University of California, Irvine, during which time I worked as a research assistant in Prof. Bruce McNaughton's lab. Motivated to learn more about mechanisms of cognition, I joined an international PhD program by NYU and NYU Shanghai, advised by Prof. Jeffrey Erlich. With zero programming experience prior to PhD, I taught myself several languages and contributed to the development of a high-throughput animal training system. I developed various behavioral tasks studying decision making in rodents, performed brain inactivations, and analysed and modelled high-dimensional data. The key courses during PhD included Bayesian modelling, machine learning and causal inference. In the meantime, I actively took online courses and earned certificates in data science and deep learning. This interdisciplinary experience forged a profound interest in the intersection of AI and neuroscience, which I am excited to pursue as part of my next intellectual journey.

  • Python libraries: NumPy, Pandas, SciKit-Learn, Matplotlib, TensorFlow, Keras
  • R libraries: dplyr, ggplot2, lme4, shiny, caret
  • Other skills: problem solving, critical thinking, experimental design, research, communicating complex ideas verbally and in writing

Projects

Risky choice task

A behavioral task where the animal chose between a small & guaranteed reward and a cue-guided risky & variable reward.

Accomplishments
  • Written in MATLAB, compatible with the Bpod system by Sanworks LLC.
  • The code communicated with Bpod state machine to trigger event-driven task states.
  • Collected precise measurement of animal behavior via infrared photogates.
  • Real-time task and event data stored in SQL database.
  • Designed different stages to train a naive animal to become a risky choice expert via reinforcing feedback.
Perceptual gambling task

A behavioral task where the animal made choices by both perceptual and value cues on a trial-by-trial basis

Accomplishments
  • Written in MATLAB, compatible with the Bpod system by Sanworks LLC.
  • Designed elaborate stages to train a naive animal to make decisions under complex audiovisual cues.
  • Animals learned the task despite a tuning-intensive training process.
  • Pushed the limit of known rodent cognition.
Modelling risky choice

Systematic modelling of the cross-species risky choice dataset using Hamiltonian Monte Carlo algorithm in Stan

Accomplishments
  • Data analysis in R, Bayesian model fitting in Stan
  • 18 models were implemented to combine different risk forms, noise specification and heuristic / trial-history mixture.
  • Hierarchical Bayesian models were implemented for some of the models.
  • A first sysmatic modelling of risky choice and a first cross-species comparison of risk-attitudes.
Smart alarm

A smart alarm system for unsupervised anomaly detection in animal behaivoral data for neuroscience labs.

Accomplishments
  • Written in python, course project for the machine learning class.
  • It first performs ForeCA (forecastable component analysis) on the noisy multivariate time series to reduce dimensionality.
  • It then performs ARIMA (autoregrdssive integrated moving average) on the individual ForeCA components to generate a prediction interval.
  • The outliers in each ForeCA component are detected based on user-specified criteria.
